# Trading Probability Basics

## Key Probability Concepts for Trading

### Expected Value (EV)
- The average outcome you can expect from a trade over many iterations
- Formula: EV = (Probability of Win × Win Amount) - (Probability of Loss × Loss Amount)
- Positive EV trades are essential for long-term profitability

### Win Rate
- Percentage of trades that are profitable
- Formula: (Number of Winning Trades ÷ Total Number of Trades) × 100
- Not as important as overall EV - a lower win rate with higher reward-to-risk can be more profitable

### Risk-to-Reward Ratio (RRR)
- Comparison of potential profit to potential loss
- Formula: Potential Profit ÷ Potential Loss
- Example: Risking $100 to make $300 = 1:3 RRR

### Position Sizing
- Amount of capital allocated to each trade
- Should be based on:
  - Account size
  - Risk tolerance
  - Trade probability
  - Maximum drawdown considerations

### Kelly Criterion
- Formula for optimal position sizing
- K% = (BP - Q) ÷ B
  - K% = Percentage of bankroll to risk
  - B = Odds received on the trade
  - P = Probability of winning
  - Q = Probability of losing (1 - P)

### Common Probability Mistakes
1. Gambler's Fallacy
2. Recency Bias
3. Overconfidence in Small Sample Sizes
4. Ignoring Base Rates

## Applying Probability in Trading
- Always calculate EV before entering trades
- Keep detailed records of win rates and outcomes
- Use position sizing appropriate to probability
- Consider multiple scenarios and their probabilities
- Never risk more than you can afford to lose
